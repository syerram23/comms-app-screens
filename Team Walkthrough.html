0 · START HERE

What you're looking at & what's inside.

Comms is an AI conversation engine — not a chatbot, not a form builder. Operators (our customers) use it to draft, send, and analyze real 8–15 minute conversations with hundreds of participants over voice, SMS, email, video, or a shareable link. Participants never install an app.

The prototype ships in two views: the design canvas (every screen laid out side-by-side for review) and 51 standalone HTML pages (one per screen, clickable, deploy-ready). This doc walks you through the second view, in the order it actually gets used.

1 · OPERATOR TOUR · 5 MIN

The shortest end-to-end loop we have.

Pretend you're Ellis at Harbor. You open Comms Monday morning. You want to send an NPS conversation to your enterprise customers by lunch. Click through these seven in order.

DEMO · A Morning loop · Home → Builder → Send ~5 min
  1. 1
    Home · Dashboard
    Your stats at a glance. Live-now shows three sessions in flight. Two drafts waiting. "Customer feedback · Q2" is running.
    WATCH FOR · the engagement bars on the three live sessions
    ~30 s
  2. 2
    Builder · New Conversation
    Click Builder in the sidebar. You'll land on an empty state with one giant input: "What are you trying to hear back?" Paste a sentence or tap a quick-start chip.
    ~20 s
  3. 3
    Builder · Drafting with AI
    After you click Draft for me, you land here. The AI has drafted the whole conversation: audience (30 ops leads), 6 steps with full prompts, plus a live SMS preview on the right. This is the canonical editable draft view.
    WATCH FOR · "A quick read on your draft" panel with AI feedback
    ~60 s
  4. 4
    Builder · Edit a Step
    Click any question card in the previous screen. A focused modal opens with the prompt, answer type, channel override, and an inline AI suggestion for a better phrasing.
    ~45 s
  5. 5
    Builder · Preview & Test
    SMS-flavored phone preview on the left, diagnostics on the right. "Projected completion 91%" · two warnings that want your attention · a "call me now" test button.
    ~45 s
  6. 6
    Builder · Dispatch
    Audience is 1,218 contacts. Channel fallback order, quiet hours, schedule. Billing preview on the right — projected cost $109.44. Big "Send to 1,218 people" button.
    ~30 s
  7. 7
    Campaigns · List
    After send, campaigns is where you watch it. Bulk-select a few rows to see the bulk-action bar appear. Click a row to see the detail drawer.
    TRY · checkbox two campaigns, bulk actions reveal
    ~30 s
2 · BUILDER · DEEP DIVE

Chat-first, with an escape hatch for power users.

This is the most-iterated surface. The first version was a node-and-wire editor — great for engineers, overwhelming for business users. We rebuilt it so the primary flow is describe it in plain English, AI drafts, you rewrite what matters.

DEMO · BChat-first path~3 min
  1. 1
    Builder · empty state
    One input, five quick-start chips (NPS · Research · Onboarding · Lead qualifier · Exit interview). Advanced-mode escape hatch top-right.
    ~20 s
  2. 2
    Builder · drafted flow
    The landing after Draft. Audience + 6 editable question cards (full prompts visible) + live SMS preview + AI feedback panel. Bottom dock: "Refine with AI" for natural-language edits.
    ~2 min
  3. 3
    Builder · edit a step
    Modal. Prompt, answer type, channel, time budget, retries, signals-to-listen-for, plus an AI "try this phrasing instead" suggestion.
    ~45 s
DEMO · CPower-user path · node editor~2 min
  1. 1
    Session Wizard (classic)
    Three-step wizard: pick a goal · choose channels · select audience. For teams who prefer forms to chat.
    ~60 s
  2. 2
    Flow Editor (node-based)
    Drag nodes around. SVG edges follow the cursor. Block palette on the left, inspector on the right. This is "Advanced mode" — surfaced via a link in the chat builder.
    TRY · grab and drag any node, the wires follow
    ~60 s

4 · PARTICIPANT · SHAREABLE LINK + AGENDA · 3 MIN

Same engine, different front door.

When someone opens the bare link (dropped in Slack, on LinkedIn, in a blast) we don't know who they are. They go through a lightweight identity gate, then enter the same agenda-driven conversation that walks through voice, video, and form steps in a predictable order.

DEMO · ECold link → agenda-driven conversation~3 min

    ~30 s
  2. 2
    Identity gate
    First name · last name · email · role · company size · hCaptcha · consent. Agenda progress dots top-right.
    ~30 s
  3. 3
    Step 1 · intro (voice)
    Agenda rail visible on the left — 7 steps. Center stage is the voice orb.
    NOTICE · the agenda rail stays put while the stage swaps
    ~30 s
  4. 4

    Participant is sharing a Google-Sheet vendor tracker. AI captions the current question, video tiles bottom-right.

8 · REAL VS MOCKED

What actually works, what's painted on.

Before wiring things up, here's the truth of what's interactive vs. what's a picture.

● INTERACTIVE

  • ✓Sidebar nav between screens
  • ✓Campaigns · row checkboxes + bulk-action bar
  • ✓Flow Editor · drag nodes, SVG edges follow
  • ✓Live · modality tabs swap the center stage
  • ✓Builder · quick-start chips pre-fill the input
  • ✓Canvas · pan & zoom (ctrl/pinch + drag)
  • ✓Participant flow · every link advances the journey

○ PAINTED ON

  • ·Form submits, captchas, "Send" buttons
  • ·Numeric data (metrics, completion %, sentiment)
  • ·AI responses (prefilled chat transcripts)
  • ·Voice/video streams (visual placeholders)
  • ·Integrations (connect buttons do nothing)
  • ·API keys (decorative — not real tokens)
  • ·Email inbox & browser chrome (rendered UI, not real)
9 · BEFORE WE SHIP

What to wire up, in priority order.

If you're scoping eng work, this is the order we should hit things. The earlier rows unblock everything that follows.

SEQUENCEWiring priorityengineering
  1. 1
    Builder · real AI drafting
    The empty-state input needs to stream a real draft into the "Builder Chat" canonical view. Everything else in this product assumes this works.
    P0
  2. 2
    Live · voice engine
    Real WebRTC + STT/TTS on the participant side. All other modalities are variants of this.
    P0
  3. 3
    Campaigns list · real send
    Campaign create → dispatch → real delivery via SMS/voice/email providers. Closes the send loop.
    P0
  4. 4
    Participant CRM · data model
    Needs the schema for a participant + events + tags before anything queries against it (segments, profile pane, audit log).
    P1
  5. 5
    Live cockpit · real transcripts
    Operator needs to see the live transcript stream in. Agent-console interventions can land after.
    P1
  6. 6
    API & webhooks · real keys
    Enables external integrations and is the contract for everything self-serve.
    P1
  7. 7
    Auth · real SSO
    Google, Okta, magic link. Relevant before enterprise but after P0 features exist.
    P2
  8. 8
    Billing · metered usage
    Per-conversation pricing from the marketing site. Needs usage metering + Stripe integration.
    P2
